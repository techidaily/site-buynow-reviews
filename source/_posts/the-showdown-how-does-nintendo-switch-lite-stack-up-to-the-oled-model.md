## **Gameplay: Not as smooth as on other platforms**

 The first real interactive thing you do with the game is to create your character, and true to other Elder Scrolls games, you can change your character‚Äôs appearance in the most minute way.  The action comes in through the fighting‚Äïan integral part of the game, as important as the role-playing. You‚Äôll be able to hone your combat abilities, whether with the magic of a mage, the arrows of a ranger, or the sword of a fighter. You‚Äôll be able to choose whether or not you want to be stealthy, sneaking up on foes, or running straight at them, axe swinging.

There‚Äôs just one issue: the open fighting system is amazing when you‚Äôre playing on PC, but on the Switch, it just isn‚Äôt as smooth. We often found ourselves struggling to face an oncoming attacker, or struggling with the motion controls to aim our bow.  Overall, that was our biggest complaint about Skyrim for the Switch. On PC, it‚Äôs actually pretty amazing just how much control you have, especially with all the mods you can download. But on the Switch, the number of buttons you have are limited, and doing things like sprinting is far more awkward than just pressing shift.

Top 25 Perfect Presents: Ideas for Your Android Devotee

What does your choice of smartphone say about you? You may be asking that question if you’re shopping for an Android user. The good news is people who like Android will probably like a wide variety of techy gifts.

The one thing you should avoid is buying them an actual Android phone or tablet. They either already have the one they want or will want to pick out a new one themselves. However, you can add some peripherals to the mix and make their Android devices work even better.

Android For the TV, Too

Justin Duino / How-To Geek

Maybe your giftee hasn’t looked into the world of Android beyond smartphones. Android is also available on various streaming devices that put apps on your TV. Here are a few of the best options available this year.

Chromecast with Google TV 4K

With a dedicated remote and the ability to stream directly from the device, the Chromecast with Google TV is a more powerful and easier to use device than the Chromecast dongle.

Best Buy

Chromecast with Google TV HD

The non-4K version of the Chromecast with Google TV offers the same experience as the 4K model, but with slimmed down specs and price.

Best Buy

Nvidia Shield TV Pro

The NVIDIA Shield Android TV Pro is probably the most high-end, powerful Android TV device you can buy. It can scale all video to 4K in Dolby Vision and play games via GeForce Now.

$199 at Amazon Best Buy

Amazon Fire TV Cube

$110 $140 Save $30

Another high-end Android option for TVs is the Fire TV Cube. It supports 4K Ultra HD, Wi-Fi 6E, Dolby Vision, HDR, and Dolby Atmos audio.

$110 at Amazon Best Buy Target

Fire TV Stick Lite (2020)

Inexpensive, discrete, and providing plenty of streaming options, the Fire Stick TV Lite is a good way to upgrade a non-Smart TV without much fuss. Yes, it runs on Android, too.

$30 at Amazon

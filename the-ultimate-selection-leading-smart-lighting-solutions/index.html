What to Look For in a Smart Bulb in 2024

Among home gadgets you can control with your phone, smart light bulbs have been around for a while. Fortunately, this means that they’ve not only gotten steadily more advanced but more affordable as well.

There are three primary considerations when shopping for smart bulbs. The first is whether the bulb produces colored or white light—or both. In general, white-only lights will be a bit cheaper than colored lights, but both styles are generally tuneable through a range of light temperatures and brightness levels.

The second consideration has to do with the receptivity built into the bulb . All of the bulbs on our list connect via Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, a hub, or some combination of all three. If a bulb connects only via Bluetooth, it’s important to remember that you’ll only be able to control it when you are within a specific range. On the other hand, with Wi-Fi-connected or hub bulbs, you can access your lighting no matter where you are through your smartphone.

Finally, you’ll also want to consider platforms. These days, most—but not all—smart bulbs connect to the major voice assistant platforms: Google Assistant , Amazon Alexa , and Apple HomeKit . If you have one of these ecosystems already established in your home, you’ll want to ensure the bulbs work well with it.

You should also be aware that smart bulbs can give you a great degree of control over your room’s ambiance. If you want the ability to turn lamps on and off either when you want or according to a particular schedule, you could consider purchasing a smart plug .

Best Smart Bulb Overall: Philips Hue

Philips is a well-established brand in the smart bulb arena, and it’s consistently delivered a wide array of reliable smart lighting products that are a breeze to set up, bright and customizable, and easy to use. With that in mind, it’s no surprise that we picked the Philips Hue Smart White Bulb as the best smart bulb for most people.

However, for full features, you have to buy a Hue Bridge hub , which lets you connect up to 50 bulbs throughout your entire home, add smart switches and motion sensors, and control lights when you’re away.

You can control the bulbs with Google Assistant and Alexa, or utilize the hub for more features and Apple HomeKit integration. Using the Hue Bridge hub also lets you set up routines, timers, and automations, which are helpful for indicating when it’s time to wake up and go to sleep. Additionally, the hub can sync your lights with movies, games, and music for a more immersive experience.

Philips Hue bulbs are some of the most expensive available, especially with the extra cost of the hub added on, but they’re widely regarded as the most reliable, intuitive, and high-quality bulbs. If you intend to invest in smart lighting for your entire home, Philips Hue bulbs are worth their higher price.​​​​​

## FAQ

Do smart light bulbs use electricity when off?

While they do use some electricity, the amount is very small . There are other energy vampires that are eating up more electricity in your home to take care of first!

### How long do smart light bulbs last?

How long your smart light bulb lasts depends a lot on how often you’re using it. Companies may quote a 10-year life , but if you have a smart light bulb on 24/7, it will not last nearly that long. Regardless, smart bulbs will last longer than regular incandescent bulbs.

How do I install a smart light bulb?

For most smart bulbs, you just need to screw the bulb into the fixture of your choice, then connect to the bulb either via Bluetooth and an app, or via a smart home hub.

Can smart light bulbs be used in ceiling fans?

Yes! Smart light bulbs can be used in any fixture without any issues.
